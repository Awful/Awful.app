EPISODE 32 - Bocage (Allied) Part 1 of 2

On today's episode we have the battle of Bocage.... I guess there was nothing more to say. ...Oh, I do have some commentary. Not sure why I had such a long pause. Enjoy the loading music.

I'm noticing the graphics are uglier than usual. For some reason certain maps come out this way in recordings more than others. Bocage in particular looks bad. View distance? Certain palettes?

I mention the importance of grenades and how, since they're limited, you should steal them from dead enemies. Seems like there's a trend in FPS's toward having automatically replenishing health and/or ammo. I think that reinforces repetitive gameplay, where conditions are always the same and you don't have to make any tactical decisions with tradeoffs. Same goes for games where your customizable kits (which should encourage creative decision-making) can let you have everything, like the Voss/rockets/medpack combo in BF2142.

Killed by a panzerschreck out of nowhere. I don't have a count for that like the "315 gets sniped" count, but that's got to be one of the more rare ways of dying.

Nice use of (unintentional?) camouflage by an enemy on the bridge.

Part 2.

By this point I've noticed that I haven't been noticing enemy aircraft much this round. I assume that our team is dominating in the air. We've held the middle flag for nearly all of the round so far, so the enemy have been bled to death: 188 to 96.

Having just written that, I now hear in the commentary that I mention those two same things: bleeding the enemy and no enemy aircraft around. It's very common in watching these videos that I think the same things that I did when I was doing commentary for them, all this time later. Funny.

You know it's a desperate situation when the enemy are forced to bring in the APC.

Wishing: the best way to call in an airstrike. In reviewing the video, the enemy tank was actually killed by either the artillery or one of our tanks. Probably arty judging by one of the youtube comments, and the fact that we control the middle flag (which spawns arty) but not our base's nearest flag (blocking the flow of tanks from our main). Battlefield detective.

A big win for a map like this. I only died three times in the round, which is good since I was doing an "in your face" combat medic role, as opposed to sniper or using a tank. Third-place guy died 23 times. The full scoreboard reveals a big difference in the teams' kill-to-death ratios. When you see that team-wide, you can bet there's a bloodbath.

EPISODE 32 - Berlin (Axis Breakout) Part 1 of 2

That screencap shows one of my very few knife kills during the LP. The knife is popular as a humiliation weapon in the game, but it's not useful like in the Call of Duty Series. In COD, you hit one key to both draw and stab with the knife, and I believe it also lunges forward automatically. And it's an instant kill. In BF1942, by contrast, you have to first switch to the knife (making yourself basically defenseless), then click again to stab. It has poor aim, and it doesn't always kill in one hit. One of the few areas where I would definitely prefer the COD game mechanics.

Besides the episode description (and the episode length), we can already see the hints that the Allies will achieve breakout. The Axis tank is dead, there's nobody ExpPacking the alleyway (or otherwise camping it), and in general there seem to be few defenders on the map. I'm going to assume at this point that this episode was filmed on a less than fully occupied server.

Though obviously a less-occupied server also means fewer attackers to slip through, I think it favors the attackers. You just need a certain amount of defenders to man the main posts and bottle up the enemy. Maybe there's an optimal point beyond which adding more people to the server starts to favor the attackers (simply overwhelming the static number of tanks), but I don't know.

As if by karma for my overconfidence in going for a knife kill, the target suddenly moves. Afterward, two flags turn gray, guaranteeing breakout.

Just as certain maps are completely different playing for Axis instead of Allies and vice-versa, Berlin is a completely different map once the Russians achieve breakout (if they ever do). That made it worth its own episode, and apparently I needed an under-populated server to show it.

This here is one of the most frustrating things you experience in an FPS: Being in a good position to ambush the enemy, who don't yet see you, then seeing multiple targets to shoot, watching them get away one by one, and then getting killed.

Looks like the stock market just crashed. I'm surprised I didn't comment on that during the episode.

Frustrated by dying so many times, I switch to the Assault class...and yeah. Back to Medic.

Part 2. We're down by 30 tickets and playing defense. The enemy's margin over us has been fairly steady since they achieved breakout and stopped the bleed; we don't have a kill-to-death ratio advantage over them. Doesn't look like this one is going to be a win.

Something like this scene wouldn't happen in Battlefield 2 due to the change in spawn rules. Everyone in BF1942 respawns at the same time. That usually benefits campers, but it can also create a frightening scene for attackers like myself...

Another rare sight, respawning as Anti-Tank because of the situation. ATs are much, much better on Battlefield 2. But it is very satisfying to one-shot a tank in the back on this game.

I argue in favor of an all-Medic team. While that would probably be superior than a random mix, I don't agree. I think Engineers, at least, do have a place on Berlin for the German team. ExpPacks are useful for camping the alleyway or booby-trapping flags. The rifle is decent for sniping chokepoints, and somebody has to deal with the enemy tanks. Landmines are effective because the roads--already narrow--can be closed off, and Engies can easily sneak up on the tanks and toss landmines directly onto them.

As the last man standing, I give up the ghost and we get capped out. I don't think that happens to my team very often in this LP...

On the scoreboard, the server was indeed a bit under-populated. But there was also heavy teamstacking: 15 on their team, 11 on ours. Interestingly, our teams had exactly identical kill-to-death ratios: 150 kills, 156 deaths!

EPISODE 34 - Battle of the Bulge (Axis Blitzkrieg) Link.

One of the most annoying things that can happen at the beginning of a round. At least put a couple jeeps near a popular vehicle for us runners-up, game designers.

Take note of the ticket count here. We just blitzed the windmill and turned a flag neutral about a minute into the round, which is about as quickly as it's going to happen. Yet the Allies have a 249-173 advantage. Usually the attacker is supposed to have a ticket lead that bleeds away unless you take a flag, and your ticket advantage/disadvantage is dependent on how quickly you were able to take that first flag. I'm guessing the settings must have been changed to make it more difficult for Axis on this map.

For some reason there are weapons that are satisfying to get kills with. It's more satisfying to get a kill using the engineer rifle than randomly shooting someone with your tank's cannon/coax. Maybe the more difficult/unexpected it is to get a kill, the better.

Some guerilla tactics used here. On BF1942 in particular, without UAVs, Q-spotting, commanders, etc. if you go undetected you can control the situation. Mostly people will charge for a flag regardless, but here I kind of make the best of things until reinforcements arrive, and I help them exploit an opening a couple minutes later. Teamwork without communication!

In the meantime, this has to be a record for compulsively switching back and forth between kits. Forget those games where they let you customize a "perfect" kit that fits every situation--like BF2142's Voss/rockets/medic combo--scavenging equipment from dead soldiers is where it's at.

I explain that I'm going to bypass the next flag with my Tiger tank and attack the back flag. This is one of the things I like about BF1942 map designs--they aren't too linear. If you want, you can maneuver around. Here, the city flag is notoriously awkward for tanks to cap on their own. If you sit there by the flag in the small cap radius, people with bazookas will spawn all around you. Most people would instinctively go for the next flag, but being willing to invest a little more time to go to the back flag can pay off.

On the bigger map, you can see the outcome of our team's other tanks' earlier attempt to take the city flag. They died. If I had helped them out, maybe we would have succeeded, but I'd much rather have Americans spawning at the city flag and getting chopped up by the next wave of tanks, rather than all spawning at their main and defending it.

The Allied main is a much nicer place to try and capture using a tank. Look how big the capture radius is. You can roll around a bit and not be such a sitting duck.

Here's a tactic I've always preferred to use, but it's not that common: Attacking an empty tank in a hostile area. A lot of people will be optimistic and leave the tank healthy, hoping a friendly will hop in it. My attitude is that unless a friendly driving the tank or right next to an empty one, it's presumed a threat and I'll blow it up (or damage it enough to discourage enemies from using it). Especially in enemy territory.

As promised, the wider capture radius allowed me and the other Axis tank to cap the flag. We now hold all the flags, which allows us to inflict bleed. Ridiculously, the enemy still have a 161-130 ticket advantage!

All enemies are dead and they have no spawns, so we instantly win. Funny enough the last kill was by someone with a knife. You can tell the timing was just perfect, because we were able to neut/cap the enemy main before their lack of other spawns sent their entire team spawning at the main. The enemy survivors got hunted down very quickly, so there weren't many of them, and they weren't far from the spawns.

A perfect round for the Axis, winning at 9 minutes. This is usually a map with longer rounds. Look at the top 3 listing. That's obviously some good tank work (though I wasn't actually in a tank until the last half): Between the three of us, 38 kills and 4 deaths. Brutal.

EPISODE 35 - Tobruk (Axis Blitzkrieg) Part 1 of 2

If you look at the preview screenshot carefully, you'll notice something different. My tank icon on the minimap is green! Several episodes into the LP, someone reminded me of the buddy list feature for the game, which I had entirely forgotten about. It's an obscure feature that requires you to go into the console. (In other words, you'd have to look it up on the internet to find out about it.)

By adding myself to my own buddy list, I'll appear in green on the minimap, making it easier to see. More importantly, all of my kill notifications will be in green. Unlike BF2, this game doesn't give extra notifications when you collect points, so this is helpful. BF2 followed in BF1942's footsteps by making squad members (including yourself) appear in green.

If I hadn't forgotten about the buddy list, I would have done this on the very first episode. Prior to posting the LP's first episode, I did at least one recording from all 21 maps. Partly this was because I wanted to see if it was even possible to get an episode for each map--I only had a couple good servers to choose from, and none of them used the full map rotation. As I feared, there were a couple maps that it took a long time to get a round on. Operation Aberdeen was the holdout. I can't remember, but I think I was ultimately forced to find an Aberdeen 24/7 server to record there. While I was hunting down all the maps, I recorded multiple episodes from some of the easier-to-find maps.

Thus, we didn't get to finally "go green" until episode 35. (I remember saying the LP has "gone green" and I suppose the phrase/trend is already dated.)

Right from the get-go, I am lucky enough to grab a tank from the uncap. Vehicles get snapped up on the Axis side very quickly at the beginning of the round. I'm sure I'll mention this in the LP commentary itself, but there are two exits from the uncap, and I like to use the northern one because the enemy doesn't defend the north as frequently. But if you want to mass armor, you've no choice but to follow what others are doing. And here, as always, the team votes southern route.

My reaction was different in the commentary, where I have been praising the team for sticking together. I guess if the majority are going to go south, the silver lining is that all of the armor went south.

Despite my tank almost being completely destroyed, I find it surprisingly easy to escape and repair. Once I begin my repairs, I can use the tank as a shield.

While neutralizing one flag, I turn and casually arc my tank shell into the slit in a distant bunker without comment. Green kill pops up. By this point in the LP, I'm pretty practiced.

After the opening of the round is over, it's no longer possible to mass armor since they'll all be respawning at the uncap at random times, as people die. So now it makes sense to take my preferred northern route.

The northern route allows you to bypass the first line. But here, instead of continuing onward, I circle back around to spawn camp the enemy. Why? Because someone else on our team already capped a flag--in fact, the second-line flag I would normally be going toward. Since we've stopped bleed, I think I'll be more effective to immediately camp/kill/divert enemy on this front-line flag.

Note how the awkward terrain and the awkward turrent angle limitation combine to make it difficult to aim at the spawning soldiers. It's almost frustrating to watch at first. But I'm familiar enough with the tank's abilities to kind of nimbly creep around the obstacles and even take the flag.

Here's a rare sight: the defending army bleeding tickets on a cap-out map. This means we have all of their flags, but some of the enemies are still alive. Normally on cap-out maps you only think of the assaulting team bleeding tickets, as they struggle to take the first flag. When defender bleed is inflicted, tickets count down more rapidly than they do against bleeding attackers.

I start delivering a post-mortem on the enemy team, which still has 160 tickets. We stopped bleed early, but were kicked back. Then we came back and held onto some flags, including, for some time, the back flag.

One of the limitations of this style of LP is that you can't see all the action. There are 32 players and I can't be everywhere at once. If I'm not with the people attacking the back flag, you're not going to see it being attacked, even if it's important to the "story" of the episode. You get to watch a "slice" of the battle. That's one of the reasons I was drawn toward doing multiple episodes of the same maps.

Judging by the awkward cut, I think this was one of the times I got caught up in what I was talking about and didn't notice the time running out, so I had to go back and dub-in "stay tuned for part 2." This post-post commentary was one of the things that left people with the impression that I was doing live commentary.

Part 2

Good example of the reason why defender bleed goes faster: It gets the round to end more quickly when you've got one hold-out (probably a sniper tucked away somewhere) who's keeping the round going. At that point half the people on his team are probably telling him to suicide anyway.

Despite a rocky start getting kicked off our beachhead, the Axis team has few casualties. I did the lion's share of the killing, with about 1/3 of the team's collective kills. The entire British team only got 30 kills this round.

EPISODE 36 - Battle of Britain (Fighter/Expacks) Part 1 of 2

The subtitle for this episode says it's partly about showing off the fighter plane. One of the areas of BF1942 I was never able to master was fighter planes. Partly that's because when I originally played the game back in 2003-04 I was on dial-up, and the target-leading issues in this game, already present even with a proper internet connection, are greatly amplified when flying. It would also help to learn flying with a good joystick, which I never had incentive to do since dogfighting while on dial-up would have been a bad way to experience the game.

Some people are absolute aces in a fighter, and they're difficult to compete with even under good conditions. Some come into the game with a lot of flight sim experience, I think. Fighter aircraft are one part of BF1942 (and BF2) where a very skilled player can become absurdly overpowered, while pilots who are decently-skilled will not inflict a similar imbalance on the game. I suspect it's difficult to balance the game mechanics because of this.

Becoming a good fighter pilot is difficult in a game with limited aircraft, because either you'll spend a lot of time plane camping, or you'll get yelled at by teammates for occupying one of the precious fighter planes. (Some servers even have rules like "don't learn to fly on this server".) If you do get into the air and the other team has a highly-skilled pilot, you'll get shot down almost instantly anyway. Yet practicing on a "live" server is a necessity to learn, even if you're just practicing on ground targets. Because of the huge factor lag plays when flying, doing target practice on single-player against bots will not help you much. You could practice on an empty server with an average ping, but you won't have moving targets to deal with.

All this is to say BF1942 (and BF2) is not conducive to developing good skills in a fighter in the same way that you can learn to be good at infantry or armor.

I'm flying using a keyboard, as opposed to a joystick. Here you see the main problem with that: The inability to set a slower speed. This makes it more difficult to spend the time you need to aim at and kill targets. On the keyboard, you press and hold the "W" key; you can only slow down by not pressing it, which causes your engines to cut off. So you're pretty much either going at maximum speed or falling out of the sky. Arguably they should have set the controls to keep a constant speed when you're not pressing the key, with W/S changing it as needed.

I talk about the psychological warfare people use in a game like this: Whining about tactics to cause others to make it easier to get themselves killed. It's effective, as I've seen people decline to bail when obviously they should. However, I do think sometimes people allow themselves to get killed in a plane as a kind of plane camping strategy: You respawn back at the airfield when you die, and can grab the plane. If you outlive the plane, someone else can take it.

I spend a little time booby-trapping planes, but circumstances don't cooperate. That's just a fact of booby-trapping, I guess...But it's a good source of laughs when it does work.

Here's a good example of "gimme" kill, practically gift-wrapped for me, yet the target-leading requirement is still very present and I almost miss out on the kill.

Finally I am practiced with leading enough to get a decent lead on the target and take it down quickly. Experts at air-to-air shooting can practically insta-pop planes, though.

A good, clear shot of the factory blowing up animation.

Part 2.

By this point in watching, I have noticed not only has there been an almost total absence of flak in the northern section (i.e. the factory and airfields, beyond the range of the British radar towers), but the lack of British fighters on my tail. I am not very good at avoiding enemy dogfighters, so the German team must be keeping them grounded well.

This is a fun sequence, using the expacks as an anti-infantry weapon by camping a spawnpoint that doesn't look closed in enough for camping. I notice for some reason my recording starts getting weird sync issues, as if in disbelief about what's happening. (Kind of like when I parachuted into the tank in that one Philippines episode.)

For some reason it's really satisfying when this works.

EPISODE 37 - The Liberation of Caen (Allied) Part 1 of 2

The decision I make at the very beginning has big consequences for the way this battle unfolds. I decide to invest extra time by taking an indirect route to my objective. I suspect the vast majority of players always head directly toward the next flag. They want to get into the action as quickly as possible!

But it's much more satisfying to take a slightly longer route and achieve the objective. If you survive instead of die, you get more action, because you don't have to respawn all the way back where you came from.

The time investment pays off as I do not get spotted while attacking the river flag. All enemy eyes are looking in the opposite direction.

This whole sequence is a great example of the power of surprise. Even as I repeatedly attack enemies, they are oblivious to my presence. They can't imagine I'm behind them the whole time. When my tank shells land and bullets hit, they assume it must be coming from the other direction. They don't realize I'm there until the flag has been neutralized and captured--and even then some don't notice!

Less than three minutes in, has that crucial capture decided the match? Bleed has stopped; we have an uncapturable base, while the Germans do not, and we're ahead in tickets.

Watching this I'm amazed by my confidence in the repair ability. My tank has virtually no hitpoints left and I am in sight of multiple infantry. Yet I just hop out, grab the wrench, and get to work trying to make the tank viable again.

Notice how few tickets we've lost. It's been about three minutes since we captured the river flag, and we've lost only 10 tickets in that time. The enemy have lost about 20.

I almost made a rare, effective use of the kit-switching ability, where you take a dead soldier's kit. I'm a bit surprised I didn't bother to run up behind the tank and mine him directly.

This sequence shows off how effective bobbing back and forth is to dodge bullets with lag. One of the soldiers gets killed by my return-fire even though he spotted me first--like in an action movie, I don't even take a hit.

Part 2

Attacking the back flag again, we're definitely in whack-a-mole mode, which is very common in this phase of Caen. But we managed to get lucky enough to cap out all the flags north of the river simultaneously.
I am accused of cheating in the chat. That's not that common, but it happens. What's more rare than a cheating accusation is when someone else in the chat backs it up. What have I done? Win a few close-quarter firefights in a row.

Running out of ammunition is the ultimate compliment in this game, especially for a medic. It means you've lived a long time.

You know it's one of those battlefield moments when something is amusing enough for me to laugh in post-commentary.

I was wondering how this match could end so soon. Luckily I had the presence of mind to bring up the scoreboard before the game cut out completely. Our team had a kill to death ratio of 2:1, so basically we kept up that same rate from right after our capture of the river flag.

315 [Mp 40] everyone
EPISODE 38 - El Alamein (B17 bomber) Part 1 of 2

I hop into the round and it's probably been going on for awhile before I arrived. With each side having already racked up over 20 kills on a slow-paced map like El Alamein, the opening phase is long past. The Brits have a majority of the flags, butthe Germans have a very significant ticket lead.

The B17 is best for striking where you already know the enemy will be, and in large numbers. Obvious result: Bomb the plane campers. You'll never lack for targets on the enemy airstrip, especially on El Alamein.

I write off all gunners on B17s as useless. Harsh, but I can't recall them ever actually doing something in all the time I played BF1942. The only time a gunner on a bomber plane gets kills is when the bomber is parked on the airfield and shoots intruders/campers.

On the third flyby, we see that the plane campers will never learn. There are actually more people spawning there now than before.

The B17 and the way it bombs is something I didn't have much familiarity with. It respawns slowly and someone always takes it at the beginning. Until I read about it in a YouTube comment, I didn't know you could drop all the bombs by holding down the bomb key. I still like spamming the bomb key though, it's fun.

When people shoot bazookas at your plane, that's a sign you're winning. I've lost count of how many times I've killed Fat Man.

I'm fianlly out of bombs. Though I'm low on hitpoints, I otherwise would have kept bombing the campers forever...

Someone asks in the chat whether I'm the guy who was in the big plane. I'm not sure why he kept asking--the kill notifications list your name and vehicle.

Even as I'm watching this, I look at the shot I take at a weird angle at the infantry and think "there's no way that's going to...oh."

As I'm enjoying commentating on the magnet for enemy jeeps, the 9-minute section of the video ends. I had to go back and re-edit the audio to interrupt myself. That happened relatively often.

Part 2

With the B17 run out of the way, it's just bonus El Alamein content. That's the thing about doing an online multiplayer LP: You can only plan things so much.

A random friendly appears to give me the thousand-yard stare. I always thought that was funny.

In a tank duel, a friendly airplane finishes off the enemy. I laughed at my reaction, which is like something from a movie "I had him!"

If you watch closely you can see a hostile jeep roll over the mountain and into the flag area. He manages to sneak up on my ally, but I get lucky and catch a glimpse and ruin his hero run.

I'm in danger as an enemy plane circles around, looking like he's going to make another run at me... But teamkills a friendly jeep instead.

Here's my MacGyver impression. Instant "Battlefield moment".

I didn't realize how infrequently I died until I saw the scoreboard. The latter half of this round was a good demonstration of how to continually maintain your tank as an Engineer. I was lucky not to get chopped up by enemy planes, but the enemy tanks couldn't do much, either.

Aces High posted:

As happy as I am to see Canadian campaigns I am a little disappointed to see that they didn't go the extra mile to equip the soldiers accurately. On the other hand it is probably expecting a lot for them to create a new model and stats for a handgun for one bonus campaign vv

The developers weren't really concerned with accuracy much. Americans use the Enfield rifle rather than the M1 Garand, except in those Japanese levels where the Japanese have their copy of the M1, the Type 5 (I know they wanted to balance it, what with the Germans and Brits using the K98 and Enfield respectively, but they could have given the Americans the Springfield 1903). Along the same lines, the Japanese use the K98 rifle as well, rather than some model of the Arisaka. Same with handguns. Beyond weapons we have a British warship at Omaha Beach, and not only that, a ship that was sunk well before the invasion began. Really, they were pretty lazy with a lot of things, not just the Canadians, though it's a fun game, so that kind of evens it out.
